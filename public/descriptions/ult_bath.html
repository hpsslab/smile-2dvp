Ultrasonic Bath

Process Description: An ultrasonic bath, also known as a sonicator, is a device used for cleaning delicate parts, substrates, and glassware with high precision. It works by using high-frequency sound waves (ultrasound) transmitted through a liquid medium. These sound waves create millions of microscopic cavitation bubbles that form and violently collapse. This process, called cavitation, generates a powerful scrubbing action that can remove sub-micron contaminants from surfaces without the need for harsh chemicals or manual brushing. Common applications include cleaning wafers, dissolving powders, and degassing solvents.

System Components:

  • Tank: The main stainless-steel reservoir that holds the cleaning liquid (e.g., deionized water, solvents).
  • Control Panel: The digital display interface used to set the sonication time and, on some models, the bath temperature.
  • Lid: A cover for the tank that reduces noise, minimizes evaporation of the liquid, and helps maintain a stable temperature.

Operation Procedure:

  1. Ensure the drain valve is closed and the tank is clean.
  2. Fill the tank with the appropriate liquid (e.g., DI water) to the indicated fill line.
  3. If a fresh liquid is used, sonicate for 5-10 minutes to remove dissolved gases, which improves cleaning efficiency.
  4. Place the items to be cleaned into a suitable beaker with a cleaning solvent, or into a mesh basket. Do not place items directly on the bottom of the tank.
  5. Suspend the beaker or basket in the bath, ensuring the liquid level inside is similar to the bath level.
  6. Set the desired cleaning time using the control panel.
  7. Press the 'Start' button to begin sonication.
  8. Once the cycle is complete, carefully remove the basket or beaker.
  9. Turn off the unit. Drain and clean the tank as required by lab procedures.

Safety Warnings:

  • Do not place your hands or any body parts into the bath while it is operating. The cavitation process can damage tissue.
  • If using flammable or volatile solvents (like acetone or IPA), they must be contained within a beaker. Never fill the main tank directly with a flammable solvent.
  • Ensure the ultrasonic bath is used in a well-ventilated area or a fume hood when working with volatile chemicals.
  • Never run the ultrasonic bath dry (without liquid), as this will permanently damage the transducers.
  • The unit generates a high-frequency noise during operation; hearing protection may be considered for prolonged use.
