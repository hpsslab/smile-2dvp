Electron Beam Lithography

Process Description: Electron beam lithography (EBL) is a maskless lithography technique that uses a finely focused beam of electrons to draw custom patterns with sub-10 nm resolution. It is the premier method for creating the highest-resolution nanostructures for research, development, and prototyping. The Elionix system uses a high-brightness thermal field emission source to generate a stable, high-current electron beam. A complex set of electromagnetic lenses, apertures, and deflectors, all managed by the control rack, allows the beam to be precisely scanned across a substrate coated with an electron-sensitive resist, directly writing the pattern pixel by pixel. System Components:

  • Electron Column & Gun: A high-voltage thermal field emission gun generates a beam of electrons, which is then accelerated, focused, and shaped by a series of electromagnetic lenses and apertures housed within a high-vacuum column.
  • Stage & Load-lock: The sample is placed on an ultra-high-precision laser interferometer stage capable of nanometer-scale movement. A robotic load-lock system allows for sample introduction and removal without breaking the high vacuum of the main electron optics chamber.

Operation Procedure:

  1. Log in to the control PC and launch the EBL control software.
  2. Verify system status, ensuring the main column and gun are at high vacuum.
  3. Vent the load-lock chamber.
  4. Carefully mount the resist-coated sample onto a sample holder and place it into the load-lock.
  5. Pump down the load-lock. Once at vacuum, transfer the sample into the main chamber.
  6. Navigate to the desired location on the sample using the stage controls.
  7. Perform system calibrations, including beam focus, stigmation, and aperture alignment.
  8. Load the pattern design file (e.g., GDSII format) and set exposure parameters (e.g., dose, beam current, step size).
  9. Run the exposure recipe to write the pattern.
  10. After the writing is complete, transfer the sample back to the load-lock, vent, and retrieve your sample for development.
  11. Return the main chamber to an idle, high-vacuum state.

Safety Warnings:

  • High voltage (many kilovolts) is present in the system during operation. Never attempt to bypass safety interlocks.
  • The system is shielded for X-ray radiation, which is generated when the high-energy electron beam strikes the sample.
  • Always leave the main chamber in a high-vacuum state to protect the electron gun and optics.
  • Push the emergency stop button in case of any abnormal system behavior or emergency.
